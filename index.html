From the Founder

FLAIR Summit London 2025 ✈
"Every brick pressed represents a step out of poverty, a home built, a family supported."
Mildred Nasipwondi Wepukhulu — Founder & Project Lead

"Welcome to Ndakaru Community Brick Manufacturing Initiative. I grew up in Ndakaru Village seeing families struggle with basic needs and lack of stable income. I believe everyone—especially women and youth—deserve a chance to build a better life with dignity.

In June 2025 I was invited to the FLAIR Summit in London (15–20 June 2025). I returned with practical training methods, safety standards, and enterprise tips that we now use to empower women and youth here at home. Those lessons are already helping our teams earn more, work safer, and mentor new members.

With this initiative, we provide more than bricks: we build hope, homes, and futures. Every brick pressed represents a step out of poverty, a home built, a family supported.

I invite you to join us—through support, partnership, or prayer—so we can expand our impact and empower many more lives. Together, we can build a stronger, more hopeful Ndakaru."

Real people behind the mission: transparent, community-led, and focused on women and youth empowerment, strengthened by lessons brought home from the FLAIR Summit London.
25 Lives Changed. 12 Homes Built. 100% Community Owned.

Grace: Production Lead

Women's Empowerment Spotlight

Before: Inconsistent farm labor income After: Steady income as Production Lead Impact: Children's school fees secured
"Because of this project, I can now earn a steady income and support my children's school fees. I feel proud of the skills I've gained and the role I play in building our community."

The Wamalwa Family

Affordable Housing Story

Cost Savings: 35% less Timeline: Completed before rainy season Quality: Strong and durable bricks
"These bricks helped us finish our home in time for the rains. We could afford them, and they are strong and durable."

Impact: The Wamalwa family saved nearly 35% in construction costs compared to conventional materials, enabling them to complete their home before the rainy season. The quality and affordability of Ndakaru bricks made homeownership achievable for this family.

Environmental Outcomes

  • Low-emission production: Our brick production avoids the burning process used by traditional kilns, significantly reducing carbon emissions.
  • Locally sourced materials: Using locally available soil reduces transportation emissions and construction costs.
  • Sustainable land-use: Our practices ensure minimal land dereliction and ecological disturbance, protecting the environment for future generations.

About Our Initiative

The Ndakaru Community Brick Manufacturing initiative produces environmentally friendly building bricks using locally available soil and manual brick presses. We are committed to providing dignified employment, skills development, and income opportunities for our community members who previously had limited economic prospects.

Our Objectives

🎯 Employment Growth

Sustain and expand employment opportunities for women and youth in Ndakaru Village and surrounding areas.

📈 Production Capacity

Increase daily brick production capacity to meet growing demand and create more jobs.

🎓 Skills Development

Train more local youth in brick making, business skills, and construction support for long-term empowerment.

Long-Term Vision

This initiative has the capacity to scale into a community construction cooperative, training center, and reliable supplier to local housing projects throughout Bungoma County.
