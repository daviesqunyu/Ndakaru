Construction Tips

5 Things to Check Before Starting Your Build in Kenya

March 2026 · 4 min read

Starting a construction project in Kenya is exciting, but a few checks upfront can save you time, money, and stress. Here’s what to verify before breaking ground.

1. Title and land ownership

Confirm you have a clear title (or lease) and that the land is free of encumbrances. A search at the lands office and your lawyer’s sign-off are essential.

2. NCA and county approvals

Ensure your project is registered with the National Construction Authority (NCA) and that you have the required county approvals and building plans signed off.

3. Soil and site survey

A simple soil test and site survey help decide foundation type and avoid surprises. Many contractors can arrange this or recommend a surveyor.

4. Design and bills of quantities

Work from approved architectural and structural drawings and a clear bill of quantities (BOQ) so you and your contractor are aligned on scope and materials.

5. Budget and contingency

Set a realistic budget and keep a 10–15% contingency for unforeseen issues. Get at least two detailed quotes from registered contractors.
