Choosing the Right Bricks for Your Kenyan Home

January 2026 · 5 min read

Bricks form the backbone of most buildings in Kenya. Picking the right type affects cost, durability, and build speed. Here’s a short guide to the most common options.

Wire-cut (red) bricks

Made from clay, fired in kilns. Strong and consistent, good for load-bearing walls. Often used in commercial and high-end residential. Cost is higher; delivery is widely available in major towns.

Machine-made (block) bricks

Concrete or cement-based blocks. Quick to lay, uniform size, and suitable for most residential and small commercial. Cost-effective and easy to source. Need proper curing on site.

Stabilised soil blocks (SSB)

Earth mixed with a small amount of cement or lime, then compressed. Eco-friendly and often cheaper where soil is suitable. Common in rural and community builds. Quality depends on soil type and compaction.
